Background

Hydrogels form the 3-dimentional network structures by cross-linking hydrophilic polymers. DNA bonds sequence specifically as it only bonds complemental bases and it is easy to design the construction of DNA since there are only four kinds of bases: adenine, thymine, guanine and cytosine. Consequently, DNA hydrogels ―hydrogels made of DNA, have attracted much attention recently as one of structural DNA nanotechnologies. DNA hydrogels make it possible to transition from gel to sol in response to photon [1], temperature [2], pH [3], physical force etc. This means DNA hydrogels have high responsivity over a wide range of physical states. Therefore, we aimed to make a DNA hydrogel whose gel condition ―whether it remains gel or transitions into sol, differs according to the patterns of input DNA strands when inputting DNA strands with certain sequence. In other words, we aimed to make a DNA hydrogel that works as a DNA AND gate. Here, we successfully made the DNA hydrogel.

[1] Kandatsu, Daisuke, et al. “Reversible Gel-Sol Transition of a Photo-Responsive DNA Gel.” ChemBioChem 17.12(2016): 1118-1121.

[2] Liu, Wenguang, et al.” A rapid temperature-responsive sol–gel reversible poly(N-isopropylacrylamide)-g-methylcellulose copolymer hydrogel” Biomaterials 25(2004):3005-3012.

[3] Cheng, Enjun, et al. “A pH-triggered, fast-responding DNA hydrogel” Angewandte Chemie – International Edition 48.41(2009): 7660-7663.
